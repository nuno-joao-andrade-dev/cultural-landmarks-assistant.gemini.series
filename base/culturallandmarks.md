# Comprehensive Guide to Beja: History, Culture, and Traditions

Beja, the heart of the Alentejo, is a city where time seems to slow down, allowing the layers of its 2,500-year history to breathe. From the Roman capital **Pax Julia** to the modern-day vibrant hub of the **Baixo Alentejo**, Beja is a treasure trove of heritage, gastronomy, and architectural innovation.

---

## Principal Cultural Landmarks

### 1. Beja Castle (Castelo de Beja)
The castle is the defining silhouette of Beja, standing on the highest point of the hill.
*   **The Marble Keep (Torre de Menagem):** At 40 meters, this is the tallest medieval keep in Portugal. Uniquely, it is built almost entirely of **marble**.
*   **Schedule:**
    *   April to October: 09:30 – 12:00 and 14:00 – 17:30
    *   November to March: 09:30 – 12:00 and 14:00 – 16:30
    *   Note: The tower typically closes 30-60 minutes before the grounds.
*   **The Legend of the Snake:** Local lore tells of a giant snake that once terrorized the town until it was killed by a poisoned bull. This legend explains why a **bull** is featured on Beja's official coat of arms.
*   **The Lidador:** The square honors **Gonçalo Mendes da Maia**, "O Lidador," a knight who died heroically defending the city at age 91 in 1170.

### 2. Rainha Dona Leonor Regional Museum (Convent of Conceição)
Housed in a 15th-century convent founded by the parents of King Manuel I.
*   **The "Portuguese Letters":** The legendary setting of the *Lettres Portugaises*, passionate love letters written by the nun **Mariana Alcoforado**.
*   **Schedule:**
    *   Tuesday to Sunday: 09:30 – 12:30 and 14:00 – 17:30
    *   Closed: Mondays and Public Holidays (Jan 1, May 1, Easter, Dec 25).
*   **Artistic Wealth:** Features a world-class collection of 15th-18th century **azulejos** (tiles) and a "golden forest" of Baroque gilded woodcarvings.

### 3. Visigothic Center (Church of Santo Amaro)
One of the most important archaeological sites in Iberia, dating to the **5th or 6th century**.
*   **Schedule:**
    *   Tuesday to Sunday: 09:30 – 12:30 and 14:00 – 17:15
    *   Closed: Mondays and Tuesday mornings.

### 4. Roman Ruins of Pisões
Located 10km from the city, this grand **villa rustica** (agricultural estate) features 40 rooms and spectacular mosaics.
*   **Schedule:**
    *   Tuesday to Sunday: 09:00 – 12:30 and 14:00 – 17:00
    *   Closed: Mondays.
    *   Note: Highly recommended to call ahead (+351 963 932 452) to confirm access.

## Modern and Institutional Architecture

*   **Liceu de Beja (Escola Diogo de Gouveia):** A landmark of **First Modernism** (1934).
*   **IPBeja (School of Technology):** Features a **50-meter floating concrete portico**.

## Gastronomy: The Flavors of the Plain

Beja's cuisine is defined by the "Holy Trinity": **Bread, Olive Oil, and Aromatic Herbs**.

### Iconic Dishes
*   **Açorda à Alentejana:** Bread soup with garlic, coriander, and poached egg.
*   **Migas com Entrecosto:** Savory bread puree served with crispy fried pork ribs.
*   **Ensopado de Borrego:** Rich lamb stew served over thick regional bread.

### Conventual Sweets (Doçaria)
*   **Porquinho de Doce (Porca de Beja):** Marzipan "pig" filled with egg and squash jam.
*   **Luiz da Rocha:** Operating since 1893.
    *   **Café/Pastry Hours:** Monday to Saturday: 08:00 – 23:00; Sunday: 08:00 – 20:00.
    *   **Restaurant Hours:** Lunch: 12:00 – 15:00; Dinner: 19:00 – 22:00.

## Cultural Curiosities

*   **Marble Giant:** Beja's castle keep is the tallest in Portugal and is built entirely of marble.
*   **The Nun's Window:** You can visit the window where Mariana Alcoforado supposedly wrote her love letters.
*   **Roman Capital:** Julius Caesar himself named the city **Pax Julia**.
*   **Floating Portico:** The IPBeja building has a 50-meter concrete roof that appears to float in mid-air.
